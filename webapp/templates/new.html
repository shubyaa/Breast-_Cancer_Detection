About Us

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that develops in the breast tissue. It is the most common cancer in women worldwide and can also occur in men, although it is rare.

Early detection is important for the successful treatment of breast cancer, and routine screening is recommended for women at average risk starting at age 50. Women with a higher risk of breast cancer may need to start screening earlier or undergo additional testing, such as breast MRI. Breast self-exams can also help detect changes in the breast, but they are not a substitute for regular screening or clinical breast exams by a healthcare provider.

The causes of breast cancer are not fully understood, but certain risk factors have been identified, such as age, gender, family history, genetic mutations, hormone imbalances, exposure to radiation, alcohol consumption, and obesity.

Treating Breast Cancer in 5 days.

Maureen shares her experience at Sharp Chula Vista’s Barnhart Cancer Center and offers important advice for women over 40: Don’t put off getting your mammogram.

  • A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein can help reduce your risk of breast cancer .
  • Regular exercise can help lower your risk of breast cancer. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity per week. .
  • Drinking alcohol has been linked to an increased risk of breast cancer. If you drink, limit your intake to one drink per day or less.
  • Early detection is key to successful treatment of breast cancer. Women should get regular mammograms starting at age 50, or earlier if they have certain risk factors.

Because Maureen’s cancer was found early, she was a candidate for a type of treatment called SAVI, which delivers radiation to the tumor site through small, flexible tubes. SAVI is finished in just five days, compared to weeks with other types of radiation treatment.
